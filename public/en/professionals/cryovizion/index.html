CryoVizion imaging system

In a single image, a clear and complete view of your patient’s postural condition.

Contact us

Cryovizion's steps

1

The patient enters an observation booth where two fluorescent LED lighting units project a bright light over their entire body. Operated by the podiatrist, two cameras then capture images of the patient’s lower limbs and entire body in various positions while digitizing their footprint. The scientifically validated1 CryoVizion postural imaging system captures and filters changes in the light reflections on the surface of the skin.

1.The Journal of Foot & Ankle Surgery 47(5) :409–418, 2008

Cryovizion's steps

2

From the images obtained, the CryoVizion software produces coloured topographies of the surface of the skin. They clearly display surface variations caused by postural, structural and musculoskeletal pathologies, as well as other anomalies. Different measurements can be carried out from the same images for an in-depth and objective evaluation.

Cryovizion's steps

3

Using the filtered images, the podiatrist communicates the results of the evaluation to the patient to help them gain a concrete appreciation of their condition, understand the diagnosis and commit to the treatment in complete confidence.

Cryovizion's steps

4

Once the recommended treatment has been carried out, the podiatrist conducts a follow-up during which he or she takes more images to assess and objectively measure the progress achieved as a result of the treatment and make adjustments if needed. The follow-up images are automatically saved in the patient file and constitute a documented history.

Postural Evaluation By Imaging
Health professionals use imaging devices and systems for a variety of medical purposes. A validated and recommended approach used by some foot doctors is to use imaging systems to more accurately evaluate and measure postural and biomechanical problems. The visual nature of this technology allows patients to more easily understand the mechanics behind their condition and its impact on their posture. WHAT IS POSTURAL IMAGING? Postural imaging is a measurement tool that helps with the diagnostic process by providing objective data in real time.
